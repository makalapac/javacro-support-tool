Customer Support Guide: Policies and Procedures

General Cancellation Process

When a customer calls or emails requesting to cancel a purchased voucher, follow these steps:

  1. Ask the customer for the voucher details: voucher number, brand, and date of purchase.
  2. Verify the purchase in the system and check the applicable brand-specific cancellation policy.
  3. Inform the customer about the eligibility of the cancellation based on the brand’s policy.
  4. If the cancellation is allowed, initiate the refund or voucher cancellation process through the system.
  5. If the cancellation is not allowed, explain the reason clearly
  6. Record the interaction and outcome in the support system for future reference.

Brand-Specific Cancellation Policies

1. BrandOne Vouchers

Cancellation Policy: BrandOne Vouchers can be canceled under specific conditions:

  • Cancellations are allowed within 48 hours of purchase, provided the voucher has not been used.
  • The value of the voucher must be less than €100 for cancellations to be processed.
  • Vouchers purchased in official BrandOne retail stores cannot be canceled, regardless of when they were bought.
  • Online purchases made via BrandOne's official website are eligible for cancellation, but those bought through third-party sellers are not.
  • If the voucher was part of a promotional bundle, the cancellation may affect other vouchers in the bundle.

2. GiftExpress

Cancellation Policy: GiftExpress allows customers more flexibility, but there are still some limitations:

  • Cancellations can be made within 7 days of purchase, irrespective of the voucher value.
  • Partial redemptions void the possibility of cancellation. If any portion of the voucher has been used, the remainder cannot be refunded or canceled.
  • GiftExpress vouchers purchased at partner locations can only be canceled through the original point of sale.

3. LuxVouchers

Cancellation Policy: LuxVouchers have the strictest cancellation policies of all brands:

  • No cancellations are permitted once the voucher has been issued. LuxVouchers are strictly non-refundable.
  • Vouchers are also non-transferable and cannot be exchanged for other vouchers or products.
  • In cases where vouchers were purchased as gifts, the recipient cannot request a cancellation.
  • Vouchers bought at luxury partner outlets cannot be returned or refunded, even if the original purchaser requests a cancellation.

4. TicketMaster Plus

Cancellation Policy: TicketMaster Plus offers cancellations under certain conditions:

  • Cancellations must be requested within 24 hours of purchase, and the voucher value must exceed €50.

5. GoVoucher

Cancellation Policy: GoVoucher offers a generous cancellation window but has certain restrictions:

  • Vouchers can be canceled up to 30 days after purchase, but only if the voucher value is under €200.
  • Vouchers purchased at third-party retailers can only be canceled if the cancellation request is made directly through the retailer, not GoVoucher's website.
  • Once a voucher has been redeemed, even partially, it is no longer eligible for cancellation.
  • If the customer received the voucher as a gift, only the original purchaser may request a cancellation.

6. Premium Deals

Cancellation Policy: Premium Deals provides the most flexible cancellation policy:

  • Vouchers can always be canceled within 14 days of purchase, regardless of value or point of purchase.
  • Once a voucher has been redeemed, even for partial use, it cannot be canceled.
  • If the voucher was purchased at a third-party retailer, the cancellation must be requested through Premium Deals' support team, not the retailer.
  • Vouchers part of holiday or exclusive sales are subject to stricter terms, and may not be eligible for cancellation after purchase.
  • If the customer disputes a charge for a voucher, they may be entitled to a full refund upon investigation, depending on the nature of the issue.

Building Entry and Security Policies

To maintain a secure and efficient work environment, all customer support staff must follow these guidelines when entering and exiting the building:

  • Identification: Always wear your company ID badge when entering or leaving the building. Security will not permit entry without a valid badge.
  • Security Check-In: All employees must pass through a security checkpoint at the main entrance, which may include bag checks and metal detection.
  • Visitor Policy: If you are expecting a visitor, ensure they are pre-registered with security. Visitors must check in at the front desk and be escorted by an employee at all times.
  • Emergency Evacuation: Familiarize yourself with the nearest exits and the emergency evacuation plan. Drills are conducted quarterly.

Workplace Setup Guidelines

Setting up your workplace correctly ensures comfort and productivity. Follow these steps when preparing your workspace for the day:

  • Ergonomic Setup: Adjust your chair, desk, and computer monitor to ensure proper posture. Your monitor should be at eye level, and your feet should be flat on the ground.
  • Computer Login: Use your assigned credentials to log in to the company system. Ensure you change your password every 60 days as per company policy.
  • Workspace Cleanliness: Keep your desk tidy. At the end of your shift, ensure all confidential documents are secured and any personal items are stored away.
  • Communication Tools: Test your phone, headset, and internet connection at the start of each day to ensure they are working properly.
  • Break Policy: Ensure you take regular breaks to maintain focus. Adhere to the scheduled break times provided by your supervisor.

Policy for Usage of Company Cars and Bikes

The company offers cars and bikes for business-related travel. Staff must adhere to the following guidelines to ensure safety and proper usage:

  • Authorization: Employees must obtain approval from their department head or supervisor before using company vehicles for any work-related travel. This approval must be documented in the company travel log.
  • Booking: Company cars and bikes must be booked in advance through the internal booking system. Always check availability before planning your travel.
  • Valid License: Employees driving company cars must hold a valid driver’s license. Bicycles may be used without a license, but staff must ensure they are familiar with local traffic laws.
  • Accident Reporting: In the event of an accident, immediately report the incident to your supervisor and fill out an accident report. For company cars, ensure proper insurance and legal documentation is provided.
  • Vehicle Condition: Inspect the vehicle (car or bike) for any damage before use. If any issues are found, report them immediately. Ensure that company cars have sufficient fuel before beginning your journey, and that bikes are in safe working order.
  • Personal Use Prohibited: Company vehicles are strictly for business-related use. Personal use is not allowed under any circumstances. Non-compliance will result in disciplinary action.
  • Safety Gear: When using company bikes, always wear the provided safety gear, including helmets and reflective clothing. For cars, seatbelts must be worn at all times.
